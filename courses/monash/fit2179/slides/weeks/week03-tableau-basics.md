# 📏 Channels: Position is King

<div class="grid grid-cols-2 gap-6">

<div>

## Effectiveness Ranking

<v-clicks>

1. **Position** (x, y) ⭐ Most accurate
2. **Length**
3. **Angle / Slope**
4. **Area**
5. **Colour (intensity)**
6. **Colour (hue)**
7. **Volume**
8. **Curvature** Least accurate

</v-clicks>

# 📝 Forum Post Example

## How to Write Your Analysis

<div class="grid grid-cols-2 gap-4 mt-3 text-sm">

<div v-click class="p-4 bg-red-50 rounded border-l-4 border-red-500">
  <strong>1. What (Data Abstraction)</strong>
  <p class="mt-2">Table dataset of country-year items. Country is a categorical key, year is an ordered temporal attribute, and site count is a quantitative value.</p>
</div>

<div v-click class="p-4 bg-yellow-50 rounded border-l-4 border-yellow-500">
  <strong>2. Why (Task & Goal)</strong>
  <p class="mt-2">The goal is to compare site counts across countries and between the two years, then summarise which country leads and where the biggest change occurred.</p>
</div>

<div v-click class="p-4 bg-green-50 rounded border-l-4 border-green-500">
  <strong>3. How (Encoding)</strong>
  <p class="mt-2">Grouped bar marks use horizontal position for year groups, vertical position and length on a common scale for site count, and colour hue to distinguish countries.</p>
</div>

<div v-click class="p-4 bg-blue-50 rounded border-l-4 border-blue-500">
  <strong>4. Critique</strong>
  <p class="mt-2">A good critique should explain one strength of the encoding choice and one limitation of what the chart cannot show.</p>
</div>

</div>

<div class="mt-4 p-3 bg-gray-100 rounded text-sm">
<strong>Checklist:</strong> What + Why + How + Critique + Story
</div>

# 📝 Forum Post Template

## Template + Example

<div class="grid grid-cols-2 gap-5 text-sm leading-snug mt-3">

<div class="bg-green-50 p-4 rounded border-l-4 border-green-600">

### Template

> This visualisation shows **[what data is shown]**.  
>
> **What:** The dataset is a **[dataset type]** with **[attributes + types]**.  
>
> **Why:** The action is **[task]** on **[target]** to **[goal]**.  
>
> **How:** It uses **[marks]** with **[channels]**.  
>
> **Critique:** A strength is **[advantage]**. A limitation is **[trade-off]**.  
>
> **Story:** The chart suggests **[takeaway 1]** and **[takeaway 2]**.

</div>

<div class="bg-blue-50 p-4 rounded border-l-4 border-blue-600">

<p><strong>What:</strong> This visualisation uses a table dataset of country-year observations. Country is a categorical key, year is an ordered temporal attribute, and World Heritage site count is a quantitative value.</p>

<p class="mt-2"><strong>Why:</strong> The goal is to compare site counts across countries and between 2004 and 2022, then summarise which country leads and which one changed the most.</p>

<p class="mt-2"><strong>How:</strong> The chart uses grouped bar marks. Horizontal position separates the two year groups, vertical position and length on a common scale encode site count, and colour hue distinguishes the countries.</p>

# 📐 Dimensions vs Measures

## Understanding Tableau's Data Panel

<div class="grid grid-cols-2 gap-6">

<div v-click>

### Dimensions (Blue)

- **Categories** — text, dates, geographic
- Used for:
  - Columns/Rows (grouping)
  - Colour, Shape, Label
  - Filters
- Example: Country Name, Year

</div>

<div v-click>

### Measures (Green)

- **Numbers** — can be aggregated
- Used for:
  - Size of marks
  - Position on axes
  - Calculations
- Example: Population (SUM, AVG, etc.)

</div>

</div>

<div v-click class="mt-6 p-4 bg-yellow-50 rounded border-l-4 border-yellow-600">

### 💬 Discussion Question 2

# 🔄 Wide vs Long Format

## The Data Reshaping Problem

<div class="grid grid-cols-2 gap-6">

<div v-click class="p-3 bg-red-50 rounded">

### ❌ Wide Format (Original)

| Country | 1960 | 1961 | 1962 | ... |
|---------|------|------|------|-----|
| China | 667M | 675M | 682M | ... |
| India | 450M | 460M | 470M | ... |

**Problem:** Cannot easily plot year on axis!

</div>

<div v-click class="p-3 bg-green-50 rounded">

### ✅ Long Format (Clean)

| Country | Year | Population |
|---------|------|------------|
| China | 1960 | 667M |
| China | 1961 | 675M |
| India | 1960 | 450M |

**Solution:** Each data point in its own row

</div>

</div>

<div v-click class="mt-4 p-4 bg-blue-50 rounded border-l-4 border-blue-600">

<div class="grid grid-cols-2 gap-6">

- Tick boxes for specific years
- Good for: Comparing 1960 vs 1980

- Range slider for year ranges
- Good for: 1960-1980 range
