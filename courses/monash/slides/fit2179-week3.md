# Forum Discussion

Use critique to make better design choices.

---
layout: fact
---

# How should we will review posts

1. Look at the chart first.
2. Identify the likely What, Why, and How.
3. Judge whether the idiom fits the task.
4. Give one strength and one improvement.

---
layout: two-cols
---

# Stronger forum posts

- identify the idiom correctly
- connect the encoding choice to the task
- explain a concrete takeaway
- critique limitations without staying vague

> Move beyond “looks good” or “easy to understand”.

::right::

# Prompt bank

1. What is the main comparison or relationship?
2. Which mark is doing the work?
3. Which channel is doing the work?
4. What would make the story clearer?

---
layout: fact
---

# From critique to improvement

- confirm the current `What`
- restate the `Why`
- suggest a stronger `How`
- change the idiom if the current one fights the message

> The best improvement is often a better idiom, not prettier styling.

# If the task changes, the chart should too

- precise category comparison: sorted bar or dot plot
- two-state comparison: connected dot plot
- full change over time: line chart
- total plus composition: stacked bar, only when part-to-whole is central

> Strong feedback explains why a better idiom fits the task better.

::right::

# Quick What, Why, How check

- `What`: the data may stay the same
- `Why`: the analytical task may shift
- `How`: bars, dots, and lines are not interchangeable

# Proposal Approval

The second half is about scope, feasibility, and readiness.

---
layout: two-cols
---

# Bring these to the discussion

- a clear topic title or question
- your draft abstract
- your Week 2 sketch
- at least two candidate data sources
- a likely story or message

::right::

# Be ready to answer

1. Who is the audience?
2. Why is the topic specific enough?
3. How will the datasets connect?
4. What is the likely story?

> If the topic is still broad, narrow it before approval.

---
layout: two-cols
---

# What I am looking for

## Approve now

- specific topic scope
- real and relevant data
- at least two usable sources
- clear public audience
- credible story direction

::right::

## Needs revision

- topic is too broad
- data is missing or inaccessible
- it becomes a dashboard instead of a story
- the audience is unclear
- the sketch no longer matches the proposal

> Approval is a hurdle step. Update the sketch if the topic changes later.

---
layout: two-cols
---

# Use your queue time well

- refine the abstract wording
- check whether the sources download cleanly
- list the main comparisons or messages
- tighten the sketch if the story still feels broad

::right::

# Do not wait passively

- sharper scope leads to faster approval
- better source links lead to better feedback
- a clearer sketch leads to better momentum
